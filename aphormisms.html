Aphormisms


13

The wise do not act; but react to the ignorant.

12

Lack of knowledge often causes conflicts.

11

The ignorant have opinions. The wise have nothing.

10

Principles grounded on the idea of equality are for the weak; no two things are the same.

9

Smoke and mirrors do not make you a god no matter how many rabbits you pull out of your ass.

8

A dictionary is a record of the past; it may guide the present but it does not dictate the present.

7

The mediocre often cannot distinguish between wisdom and folly.

6

Religion is as good as a baby walker; you must learn to walk without it.

5

Confidence can be a symptom of ignorance.

4

An inclusive system is inclusive not because it welcomes everyone but because it treats those outside the way it does those inside.

3

Meet your heroes; values built on partial-truths become antithetical.

2

The system which a shepard imposes on the sheep must not be imposed on the dog for the sheperd relies on the dog to herd the sheep.

1

A snailfish does not want to care about the difference between a vulture and a bat though they are all connected to each other.

0

The difference between me and Friedrich Nietzsche is that I have had access to copies of Hindu and Buddhist teachings with fewer mistranslations.
