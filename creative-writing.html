Bag of Eyes

It is said that in Ooreville, which lies on the hill Kathihill, a cursed sword stands on the cliff that no man dares touch for it may strip them of the light in their minds.

This I can believe for I had an uncle, when I was a child, who visited the town to study the phenomenon. Later that night, he was seen walking off the cliff.

Though some believe the sword fell from a world beyond our own, the scriptures allege it belonged to a man whose face the world shall forever be a stranger to.

The heavens whispered, in his dreams, of a fair maiden bound in a tower on the cliff. The stars tethered his heart to her. He swore to find her and free her. His destiny awaited.

Stars drifted across the nights as he travelled across many lands, some of which were known only to a few. He carried with him a sword and a bag filled with the eyes of those that dared cross his path.

On the walls of the tower sat a shadowy figure whose nature was not of the known world. It seemed to be a sort of monster, or a shadow of a monster, of a form only an ill mind could dream up. Its form resembled parts of moth and deer. An antlered head surmounted a stretched body with ripe wings. Its ragged figure, darker than night, hazier than dreams, twitched and flickered like flames.

The blade passed right through the misty void that it was. There was no bloor nor hurt.

With a penetrating gaze, the man marched towards the door and right through the void. Each breath cost him dearly his strength, and will had faded from his body. His chest, on fire, grew heavy. A crushing ache crowned on his head. Consciousness waned. Had he not turned around as soon as he did, the void would have consumed him.

A few moments passed before he caught his breath and gathered enough strength. Upon brief contemplation, he dashed right back in with a speed that was nearly not meant to be.

No matter how great his speed was, nor how long he ran for, he came no closer to the door. Soon he was back out trembling like a featherless bird.

How would you kill what you could not touch?

He sat under a tree and contemplated the horizon as it swallowed countless suns. Memories of those that had set him on that path gathered for a summit.

An ocean of voices condensed all around him. They are but mirages in which he drowned. The air turned to cuttlefish. A subtle pattern of warm chaos spiralled. In its eye, he lay paralysed, gasping for breath

In the distant corner of the summit was a bar in which a man sat with a bottle, grieving his lately missing eye. A chuckle silenced the memories. In the newfound quiet, the heavens whispered once again.

The sword rested by a stone and footsteps approached the door to the tower. The floating abyss swirled and flickered but did not suffocate.

Silence greeted him upon entering the tower. He paced up the stairs to the chamber at the top. Cold air turned stale and his breath numb. A strange yet familiar face greeted him. He fell on his knees, and a river ran down his face. He had finally found whom he had come to rescue. In the middle of the naked room, there stood a dust covered mirror.

Hymn to the Moon

A young lass sat in a garden of stars. A constellation of fireflies flocked around her. A spell of breeze stole her veil away. Her face to the parted sun. Freckles on the cheek. A bite on the neck. A subtle glance at the heavens. Tattered clouds resembled the beach at dawn. Bare foot, a stroll down the coast. A crescent shining shell peered through grains of cloud. How I wish I could carry it in a jar of glass.

Fiery clouds scattered down the horizon. The sky- red as a dragon's breath. From a slumber awakened the beast - cosmic in nature, astronomical in scale. Lids humbly parted over the eye that robbed the sky of its light and bled through a cloak of clouds. She pulls on my gaze yet she lingers away. The wind whispers in her voice. The ocean stumbles under her breath. Her gaze- paralysing; her touch- subtle yet professing; her kiss- deadly.

Grey Paradise

A gloomy river of clouds flooded the blue sky. Piercing wind carried the scent of death as eagles soared with an appetite. A plague of pigeons scrambled unceremoniously; they circled back to the start and gathered as they were. Thrice the eagles have returned- wave after wave, they missed their prey. Upon being left alone by their predators, the pigeons sought company among their own. The mating rituals soon have been interrupted by the cold showers from the west.

Birds of the Sky

Strange, winged creatures stood still in the shade of a cracked wall. Their feathers were of colour akin to that of wood under rain. Their eyes were dense as the void; a constellation of tiny specks glowed from within.

A child approached the creatures with a bowl of seeds. He sat with his back to the wall and let out a breath of prayer. As he tossed a handful of seeds on the ground in front of him, the creatures began to move. In the midst of the crowd was a youngling. From beneath its eyelids, a thin layer of skin crawled out, masking the void. The creature flew up to the bowl and indulged itself. The rest waddled around pecking at the ground in remarkable unison.

Later that night, incomprehensible noises flooded the dreams of the child, turning them to cryptic nightmares. He woke up only to find the noises still haunting him. The source, he realised, was outside.

Motivated by curiosity and fuelled by annoyance, he moved towards the door to the balcony. With every step, hesitance slowed him further, his curiosity grew stronger, his heart beat louder.

An eternity passed; he turned the knob and pushed the door wide open. An obscure being with a translucent form sat among dense clouds. A swarm of lightning traversed around it in ways unnatural.

Ripples of screeches echoed out of the dark of the night. Dense little shades moved in front of the clouds. The winged creatures were as fast as their screeches. They moved with the grace of a dancer and the swiftness of a fighter. The ripples of screeches collided with the swarm of lightning, denting the very fabric of reality around them.


Liminal Express

The glass, tinted unevenly, did not open. On the other side are a great many windows placed symmetrically on walls of unsaturated colours. I could not count them all if I had an eternity.

At a distance, a group of younglings, full of life, stood tall waving their hands at the speeding train. I imagine they must have not seen me, for I am behind a tinted glass. Would it have been of any consequence to return the wave?

The Gods painted the heavens with a symphony of colours. Never have I laid the slightest glance upon a spectacle so terrifyingly breathtaking.

Something peeked out of the sky. Its skewed body, scattered edges, and obscure being mocked me.

"Does that look like a rabbit to you?"

A strange yet comforting voice stole my senses. I wonder why I wasn't expecting company. To my side is a child whose attention could not be taken off the clouds.

The hind legs make it look more like a hare than a rabbit.

"Sure," I replied.

I gazed beyond to find a thin, subtle yet paralysing stroke of violet bordering a thick fiery cloud. With all its might, the sky, on fire, roared bright.

What does that one look like to you?

In the Mirror

Little Lenny pulled a .44 Magnum from his wrinkled robe, demanding to know who the "snitch" was. Helicopters circled the mansion, and the special ops were closing in. The new kid dropped to the ground with a new hole in his head. I knew that I was the last person that Lenny would suspect, but I needed his men alive. I realised what I had to do even though it was against the protocol. I blew my cover, and I apprehended Little Lenny. Before his men could successfully put a bullet in me, special ops stunned them. You should have seen the look on Lenny's face- he was speechless, realising that I was not who he thought I was. It was not the first time that I have seen that look on someone. In fact, it was the same look that I have when I see faces staring back at me in the mirror.

Civilisation

On the midnight of 13 April 2919, our broadcast had been answered by a particularly intelligent alien species. They sent us their own "Arecibo message."

It took us nearly a decade to decipher their message. When we did, the experts concluded that the alien civilisation was a Type II on the Kardashev scale, and recommended terminating the communication.

If history had taught us anything, it is that the encounters between civilisations with advanced versus primitive technologies have gone badly for the less advanced.
