Education as problem solving


If you cannot efficiently solve a problem with the knowledge and perspectives you possess, acquire more knowledge and perspectives.


Introduction

Imagine sudoku. You know that at the end, each number should exist only once in each row, column, and section. To fill the cells, you take into consideration the numbers which are already present. You analyse and decide where to place a number.

Imagine teaching. You know that a student should acquire certain skills or knowledge by the end of a class or course. To educate them, you take into consideration their abilities and a few other things. You analyse all the factors and decide how to teach.


Possess Knowledge

To solve a sudoku puzzle, you must be able to read and recall numbers, know how to think about the information that is available and missing.

In order to teach efficiently, you must be acquainted with a lot of knowledge from many fields including psychology, philosophy, and sociology.


Adapt to Changes

Imagine the puzzles are appearing in different languages. You learn more languages to be able to read and enter numbers.

Various challenges are faced by educators. You must learn to analyse these challenges and use reasoning skill to overcome them in the most peaceful and productive way possible.

Knowledge regarding problem solving can be acquired by continuously learning from everyone and everything including books, journals, seminars. Learn from multiple sources of knowledge including your juniors - you may know more than your juniors, but they may know at least a few things which you do not, and those few things could help you a lot. Your own attitude should not be a barrier to learning. The end goal is to solve problems. Prioritise that and act on it.

The mind is a tool which is not to be taken for granted. Books and other things help speed up learning process, but if you can think systematically and contemplate the nature of things, you may be able to solve a novel problem with relative ease.


Recommended Action

1. Whenever something goes wrong, instead of looking for excuses or for someone to blame, ask yourself what you can do to make things better in a peaceful and productive manner.

2. Talk to everyone, learn from everyone and everything, especially from those with lifestyles different from yours. It is when you expand your interaction beyond your close circles that you learn to think and see things in various ways and solve problems more efficiently.

3. Visit places with completely different cultures or languages. Stay kind, patient, and open minded with others and yourself. Mental fixedness is a barrier to problem solving.


Proof of Concept

When I was an intern, two of my students got into a conflict with each other. I reminded them of their interests and ambitions (which I asked them about earlier in order to get to know them); about the mindset and behaviour they ought to possess in order to achieve their goals (Having an aim in life helps maintain composure and discipline) I informed them that I was not scolding them or punishing them (in order to preserve rapport and their emotional well being) but that I was only telling them something important to remember throughout their lives. I pointed out that their actions did not solve any problems, and taught them that it is okay to experience feelings; that it is important to stay calm and think things through before doing something; that they should talk to each other peacefully and ask themselves what they need to do to solve the problem at hand. I taught them what they needed to do the next time a similar conflict occurred. Later, a similar conflict occurred and they handled it in a calm and mature manner without letting emotions take over. MISSION ACCOMPLISHED!

To inspire students, consistency in behaviour is important. If you tell your students that they should stay calm but you act with force, think ill of them, or speak ill of them behind their backs, they may not develop desirable attitude or behaviour. Students notice everything, and the inconsistencies in your reactions, attitude, or behaviour are often not so subtle to their eyes.


Steps of Problem Solving

1. Identify & analyse the scenario.
How is the given scenario different from the expected standard?

How much damage is being caused due to the variation from the standard?

How quickly should this variation be addressed?

2. Describe the scenario.
Clearly and concisely describe the scenario to understand what the end goal is.

3. Identify the factors of the scenario.
What caused this variation from the standard?

Can it be addressed in such a way that it will not happen again?

4. Develop potential ideas to address the scenario.
What can be done to adjust the variation?

What better alternatives are there to the idea I just came up with?

5. Implement and Evaluate.
Execute the ideas and evaluate the effects.
