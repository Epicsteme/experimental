Creating Interests Among Students


To like something, you must first be aware of its existence.

Pleasure-pain principle refers to the tendency to avoid pain or seek immediate pleasure. When a student learns a subjects, it is due to their interest in the subject or the fear of punishment.

A student who scores high grades in a subject may not choose the same field at college level or career level if they are not interested in it.

Children are likely to grow interest in or follow the paths of their family or friends due to frequent exposure.

To create interest in something, exposure is necessary. For example, to create interest in firefighting, a firefighter could be called to the school along with their fire truck. Students should be made to interact with the firefighters and learn about their work.

You may also bring items to class and let students interact with them to create interest in related field. For example, when a student uses a microscope, they may develop an interest in microbiology.
