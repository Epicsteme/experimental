Using schemas to teach new concepts


Students learn by connecting new knowledge with knowledge and concepts that they already know, thereby constructing new meanings.

When I was teaching about water resources, the word "regulate" came up and the students didn't know what it meant. I then pointed to the fan regulator on the wall and asked them what it was and what it did. The students explained that it was the fan regulator and that it controlled the fan. I pointed out that it was used to regulate the fan. I then asked them what it meant to regulate water.

Since they saw and used the fan every day, they had a schema for the fan and its regulator. I used their existing schema (fan regulation) to help them build a new schema (water regulation).
