Offer easy targets to motivate students


Completing an easy task offers encouragement to take up a harder task.

To improve reading skill, I told my students to read a specific text and ask to clarify their doubts. A student seemed uninterested in the activity as he found it challenging to read the text. Instead of forcing him, I rephrased the instruction and told him to ask me five doubts. He promptly started reading and asking doubts as expected.

Instead of forcing the student to follow an instruction, I reconsidered my approach and offered a variation of the instruction with a clear and relatively easy target that he could see himself accomplishing in a short span of time. The next day, I increased the number of questions I expected him to ask and he accomplished his goal that day.
