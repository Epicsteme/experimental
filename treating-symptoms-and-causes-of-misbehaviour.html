Treating Symptoms and Causes of Student Misbehaviour


If you cannot efficiently solve a problem with the knowledge and perspectives you possess, acquire more knowledge and perspectives.


The Incident

During a class, I noticed a student (13/M) yelling at the students who were sitting near him. This particular student had a reputation for being "mischievous." Instead of instructing him to be quiet, I asked him why he was yelling at them. He said that they were calling him derogatory names. The other students did not deny this.

I told him that he should not react to people when they speak ill of him or try to provoke him. I told him that he should not give power over himself to others; that if he reacts to everything others say, he would always be under someone else's control. I told him to inform the teacher the next time someone calls him names, and that he should respond to such people only when his work is being affected.

I spoke with the other students and taught them to be kind to their classmates and help them when in need instead of making fun of them; that building a reciprocative relationship would benefit everyone.


Evaluation of the Incident

Though I was momentarily bothered that the student was disturbing the class, I chose to remain calm and find out what was happening and why. It turned out to be a good thing to enquire the students regarding the matter which allowed me to understand the whole picture and give appropriate guidance to the students.


Understanding the Causes

In order to understand what caused the students to behave the way they did - a group of students calling names - a student taking offence and reacting - you have to be familiar with various psychological factors, among other things.

Bullying is often a result of inferiority complex, inadequate affection, inadequate attention, dysfunctional families, peer influence, etc. To understand which factors cause students to call someone names, you need to know the background of the students. You also need to figure out which student is inspiring others to bully and why others choose to follow.

In the incident mentioned, there was one particular student who was the most influential of the group. I spent some time talking with him and learned about his interests, hobbies, friends, family, life goals, and a few other things. This student came from a troubled family and he lacked healthy role models outside of school. Being a trainee teacher, there was not much I could do to guide his family. I made some comments and suggestions regarding his interests and life goals in order to guide him, and to develop better rapport and a more common ground where he could feel that he could connect with me. Since then, he began to show better progress in academics and social life.


Conclusion

I thoughtfully addressed an undesirable incident in classroom and took actions which resulted in the students showing better progress in academics and social life.

It is important for you to learn as much as possible about psychology as well as how to talk to people. Read a lot of psychology books, journals, web blogs, watch seminars, observe and talk to everyone, especially those with lifestyles and habits different from yours. Most importantly, be kind, patient, respectful, and open minded.
