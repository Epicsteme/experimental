Treating Symptoms and Causes of Student Misbehaviour


If you cannot efficiently solve a problem with the knowledge and perspectives you possess, acquire more knowledge and perspectives.


The Incident

Once a student (13/M) was yelling at those who were sitting near him. This particular student had a reputation for being "mischievous." Instead of instructing him to be quiet, I asked him why he was yelling at them. He said that they were calling him derogatory names. The other students did not deny this.

I told him that he should not react to people when they try to provoke him; that if he reacts to everything others say, he would always be under someone else's control. I told him to inform me if someone calls him names again. I told the other students not to do it and addressed them again after the class.


The Causes

Bullying is discussed among various theories such as personality theory, dominance theory, organisational culture theory, humiliation theory, socio-cultural theory. To understand which factors cause students to call someone names, you need to learn about the students and their background. It is also important to understand the social dynamics of the class- figuring out which student is initiating an action and who is following them.

There was one particular student who was the most influential of the group. I spent some time talking with him and learning about his interests, hobbies, friends, family, life goals, and a few other things. Being a trainee teacher, there was not much I could do to guide his family. I offered some suggestions regarding his interests and life goals in order to guide him and to develop better rapport so that he could feel he could connect with me. Since then, he began to show better results in academics and social life.


Conclusion

I thoughtfully addressed an undesirable incident in classroom and took actions which resulted in the students showing better progress in academics and social life.

To effectively deal with undesirable behaviour, it is important for you to learn as much as possible about psychology as well as how to talk to people. Read a lot of psychology books, journals, web blogs, watch seminars and public speeches, observe and talk to everyone, especially those with lifestyles and habits different from yours. Most importantly, be kind, patient, respectful, and open minded.
